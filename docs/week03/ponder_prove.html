CSE 382: Week 03

Tasks

Your Tasks

Task 1

Write and test a custom implementation of the map functor called c_map (see Figure 2 for the description).

Task 2

Apply c_map to encipher and decipher a piece of text containing only capital letters and spaces using a Caesar cipher.

Task 3

Use the map BIF to map a large list. Compare the speed and the accuracy of the two implementations. (HINT: Google how to time code snippets in the language you are using. Also make sure your run your speed test several times.)

Task 4

Write and test a custom implementation of the filter functor called c_filter (see Figure 3 for the description).

Task 5

Apply c_filter as a sieve to find all of the prime numbers from 2 to 20,000. (HINT: You may need to write a helper function that performs the Sieve of Eratosthenes or some other sieve of your choice.)

Task 6

Use the filter BIF to sieve to find all of the prime numbers from 2 through 20,000. Compare the speed and the accuracy of the two implementations.

Task 7

After completing Tasks 1 through 6, find the source code for the BIFs for your chosen language and compare them to your implementations of the c_map and c_filter functors. Speculate, in writing, as to why the standard implementations differ, or not, from yours.

AFTER you have completed and debugged your code, take a look at one of many possible solutions and compare it to the code you wrote.
