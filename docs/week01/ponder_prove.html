CSE 180: Week 01

Tasks

Your Tasks

You have three tasks this week.

Task 1: Make and Join a Team

Your first task is to join a team and inform the teacher who is on your team. Only one member of the team needs to inform the teacher. Do this BEFORE Friday’s class.

To be able to wisely form a team, it is important for you to get to know your classmates and find a few you wish to collaborate with during the semester. Use the discussion tool your instructor has chosen to tell others about yourself. Include the same kinds of information you would want to know about them so all of you can make an informed decision about who to team-up with. Mention your strengths and weaknesses (ex. programming, using unfamiliar technology, researching answers, abstract thinking).

Your initial post should be a few paragraphs long. Include in your posting a list of when you are available outside of class (possible meeting times of day and days of the week).

Use these postings and the discussion tool to arrange and join a team. Remember to inform your teacher who is on your team.

You and your team members will be accountable to each other for the learning activities you do outside of class. This accounting and peer-assessment will happen regularly. Expect to spend about 6 hours outside of class each week doing readings, writing code, and completing other tasks. You will need this amount of time-on-task to be able to make a good accounting to and support your teammates.

Task 2:

Read Elder Oak's general conference talk in it’s entirety. Read the portion of Barney and Maughan’s philosophy article relative to Complexity Theory. Take notes on both that compare and contrast the cold syntax and phrasing of the latter with the warmth of the former. Such is the difference between philosophy and the gospel of Jesus Christ.

You can find the links to these two items in the reading for this week.

Task 3:

Meet outside of class with your team to discuss and compare notes on the readings. Strive for a rich discussion.
